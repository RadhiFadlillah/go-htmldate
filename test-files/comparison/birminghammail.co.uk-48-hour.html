48-hour order in Walsall after trouble flares and police cars 'pelted with bricks'

    A 48-hour dispersal order has been brought into place across four Black Country estates after youths pelted police cars with bricks. The vehicles were left damaged in the Coalpool area of Walsall and residents claimed their vehicles have also been damaged.

    West Midlands Police has brought in a 48-hour dispersal order for Coalpool, Goscote, Harden and Blakenall after trouble flared last night, November 2. Officers have been granted more powers, allowing them to disperse anyone causing anti-social-behaviour or being a nuisance.

    The police force branded the behaviour 'totally unacceptable' and warned officers are 'determined' to put a stop to it. The dispersal order has been granted under Section 35 of the Anti-Social Behaviour, Crime and Policing Act 2014 and it covers the following roads: Goscote Lane, Coalpool Road, Blakenall Road, Harden Road and parts of Bloxwich Road.

    READ MORE: Get a free National Trust pass worth up to £50 for your family day out this autumn

    A spokesperson for West Midlands Police said: "We've put a dispersal order in place in the Coalpool and Goscote area of Walsall tonight. Over the last few days we've received a number of calls from the public about people causing anti-social behaviour in and around the Coalpool area of Walsall.

    "We've had several police cars damaged after bricks were thrown and members of the public have also reported damage to their vehicles. This behaviour is completely unacceptable, the people involved are causing issues in the local community and we are determined to stop this happening.

    "Over the next 48 hours we've put in extra policing powers which will allow us to disperse anyone engaging in anti-social behaviour and causing public nuisance. The section 35 dispersal order will cover areas of Coalpool, Goscote, Harden and Blakenall.

    "This includes Goscote Lane, Coalpool Road, Blakenall Road, Harden Road and parts of Bloxwich Road. We will also continue to investigate the crimes associated with the issues being caused in the area and will be taking action against anyone involved.

    "If anyone has any information that could help our investigation then they are urged to call us on 101 quoting log 3697 2/11/23. Alternatively they can leave information anonymously by calling Crimestoppers the independent charity on 0800 555 111."
